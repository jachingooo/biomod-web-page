Function

Function

The biggest problem with DNA nano-origami technology currently lies in manufacturing costs and in vivo stability, which are also the focus of international research.Our method provides the possibility of providing low-cost and more stable nano assembly structures.

Provide a pathway for large-scale aggregation of Z-DNA specific binding proteins

DNA ring hybridization produces antibodies such as Z-DNA, Z22, and ZBP1. The structure designed by our team can aggregate a large number of Z-DNA specific binding proteins

Provide raw materials for studying Z-DNA

The structure designed by this team contains a large number of Z-DNA structures, which can generate a large amount of Z-DNA, providing raw materials for studying Z-DNA.

Software assisted sequence design and structural prediction

The software designed by our team facilitates our sequence design, reducing the burden of handwritten sequences and testing secondary structures.


Future

Developing hybrid approaches from other perspectives

At present, we have designed sequences that can hybridize dumbbells at 0, 90, and 180 degrees. In the future, we will also design sequences that can hybridize at 30, 60, 120, and 150 degrees.

Building a three-dimensional structure

At present, the team is designing sequences for the purpose of building flat graphics, and in the future, the team will attempt to design dumbbell structures and sequences for the purpose of designing three-dimensional structures.

Provide components for molecular robots

In the future, when designing molecular robots, dumbbell hybrid structures can be used.

Future

Store picture into DNA

We have implemented simple image encoding schemes which are currently only suitable for monochrome patterns. We will continue to explore and experiment with the encoding of complex images like 3D pictures and do some experiment to verify.

Store Chinese books into DNA

According to our current design, it is already possible to store an entire book into DNA sequences. We will continue to improve the encoding method to reduce the base error rate and achieve our final design goals. At the same time, we will continue to explore the relevant experimental conditions and store more information in DNA sequences to verify the universality and versatility of the overall design. We expect to achieve the storage of The Si Ku Quan Shu (Complete Library in the Four Branches of Literature).

Store the whole library which contains different books in different languages into DNA.

In the future, we can modify our encoding rules to accomodate a wider variety of languages and expressions.

We also expect to "read" DNA libraries under lower experimental conditions or even normal conditions one day.
